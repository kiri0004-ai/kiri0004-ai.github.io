Over the past few years, I have been balancing both work and school, gaining valuable experience in
responsibility and time management. I began working as a porter while I was in the 10th grade, which
gave me my first introduction to the working environment. In 11th grade, I took on an additional job as a waiter,
further developing my communication and customer service skills.

Alongside these jobs, I also pursued a small entrepreneurial project by buying second-hand laptops,
repairing and refurbishing them, and then reselling them for a modest profit. This activity not only helped me
develop technical skills but also gave me insight into business, problem-solving, and handling transactions independently.

Just three days after graduating from high school, I moved to the Netherlands to begin my next chapter.
I started with a summer job to help cover part of my university and housing expenses. Shortly after,
I began studying at HZ University of Applied Sciences.
